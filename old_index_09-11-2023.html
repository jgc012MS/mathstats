Step into my world, a tapestry woven from the vibrant hues of the San Fernando Valley, my immigrant heritage, and the transformative influence of key figures in my academic journey. At 20, I faced severe circumstances, battling an illness that reshaped my perspective on life. Concurrently, Mrs. B, a dedicated community college teacher, ignited a spark in me for mathematics. These pivotal moments, combined with her guidance, set me on a path of discovery and resilience. This journey culminated in my collaboration with Dr. John Krueger, my esteemed advisor, under whose mentorship I completed my Ph.D. and contributed significantly to the field of set theory. As the son of immigrants, my narrative is one of overcoming challenges, relentless ambition, and an unyielding quest for knowledge, taking me from personal adversities to the corridors of academia.


Quantitative Science and My Journey

My mathematical odyssey was not typical. From grappling with Algebra 2 in high school to becoming a published mathematician, my trajectory has been both challenging and rewarding. Collaborating with Dr. John Krueger, we ventured into the intricate realms of forcing and trees, leading to groundbreaking insights. Beyond academia, I'm driven by a passion for nurturing and mentoring. As an educator, I inspire students to immerse themselves in the wonders of math and research.

Paper Summary

In collaboration with my advisor, Dr. John Krueger, our paper, "Some Results on Non-club Isomorphic Aronszajn Trees," delves into the construction of Aronszajn trees with robust rigidity properties. Harnessing the diamond principle, we introduced a family of distinct trees and expanded upon seminal results to higher cardinals.

Citation

Jose Chavez & Dr. John Krueger. "Some Results on Non-Club Isomorphic Aronszajn Trees." Notre Dame J. Formal Logic 63 (1) 109 - 120, February 2022. [Link](https://doi.org/10.1215/00294527-2022-0007)
